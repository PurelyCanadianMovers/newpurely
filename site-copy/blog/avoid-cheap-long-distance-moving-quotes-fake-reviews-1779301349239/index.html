Do not fall for cheap long-distance moving quotes or fake reviews

Lowball moving quotes and polished review profiles can hide expensive surprises. Learn what to watch for before you trust a mover with your belongings.

Moving, especially long-distance moving, is a major life event. It is exciting, but it can also be stressful. When you are planning, packing, and budgeting, a very low moving quote can look tempting. Everyone wants to save money.

But when a long-distance moving quote sounds too good to be true, it often is. Unscrupulously cheap quotes can hide added costs, poor service, or even outright scams. Online reviews can also be difficult to judge when fake praise, review spikes, or vague ratings make a company look more reliable than it really is.

The red flags of cheap long-distance moving quotes

When you compare quotes for a long-distance move, be cautious with estimates that are dramatically lower than the rest. A fair price is one thing. An abnormally low price can signal trouble ahead.

Unrealistic lowball estimates

Reputable moving companies factor in labour, fuel, equipment, insurance, route planning, valuation coverage, and operating costs. If one quote is far below every other estimate, the mover may be inexperienced, cutting corners, or planning to add unexpected charges later.

No in-home or video estimate

For long-distance moves, a professional mover should complete a careful assessment of your belongings. That may be an in-home visit, video survey, or detailed inventory review. Without that step, a mover may underestimate the weight or volume of your shipment and then increase the price on moving day.

Large upfront deposits

A small booking deposit may be normal, but be very careful if a mover demands a large upfront payment before providing service. Large deposits can be a warning sign, especially if the company has limited contact information, vague paperwork, or no clear local presence.

Vague or non-binding quotes

Ask whether the estimate is binding, written, and based on a real inventory. A vague quote with unclear language can leave room for price changes, fuel surcharges, stair fees, elevator fees, or reweigh charges later. If a mover will not explain the estimate clearly, proceed with caution.

No physical address or licensing information

Legitimate moving companies should have verifiable business information, proper insurance, and a physical presence. If a company only uses a cell phone, a generic email address, or avoids basic business questions, that is a serious warning sign.

How to spot fake online moving reviews

Online reviews can help you choose a mover, but they can also be manipulated. Look beyond the star rating and read the pattern of reviews carefully.

Look for extremes and missing details

  • Too positive or too negative: Reviews that are extremely enthusiastic or extremely angry without specific details may be suspicious.
  • Generic language: Comments such as "great company" or "very professional" are less useful when they do not mention the route, crew, timing, packing, delivery, or problem resolution.

Review the profile behind the review

  • New profiles: A one-review profile may be legitimate, but many new profiles posting similar praise can be a red flag.
  • Odd review patterns: If a profile reviews several moving companies in a short period with similar wording, be cautious.
  • Location mismatch: A review should make sense for the mover's service area and the type of move described.

Watch review timing and wording

A sudden surge of positive reviews after months of inactivity can be a sign of review manipulation. Identical wording across multiple reviews is another warning sign. Real customers usually describe their move in different ways.

Read the company's responses

How a mover responds to reviews matters. A professional company should address concerns respectfully and offer practical help. Defensive, aggressive, or absent responses can reveal how the company may treat customers when a move becomes difficult.

Compare multiple review platforms

Check Google, BBB, Facebook, and other review sources. If one platform looks perfect but others show serious unresolved complaints, investigate further before booking.

How to choose a reputable long-distance mover

For a safer long-distance move, focus on reputation, transparency, and experience instead of chasing the lowest number.

  • Proper insurance and valuation options: Make sure you understand what protection is included and what additional coverage is available.
  • Transparent pricing: A reputable mover should explain the estimate, what is included, and what can change the final cost.
  • Detailed, consistent reviews: Look for specific customer experiences across several platforms.
  • Experience with long-distance moves: Cross-province moves require planning, equipment, timing, and route knowledge.
  • Professional communication: The estimate process should feel clear, respectful, and organized.
